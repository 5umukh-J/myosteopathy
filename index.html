What is Osteopathy?

A holistic approach to healing & movement

Osteopathy is a form of drug-free, non-invasive manual medicine that focuses on total body health by treating and strengthening the musculoskeletal framework. It recognises the important link between the structure of the body and the way it functions.

Osteopaths use their hands to diagnose, treat and prevent illness and injury, applying a range of techniques such as soft tissue massage, joint articulation and manipulation, and cranial osteopathy.

Drug-freeEvidence-basedHolisticNon-invasiveAll ages

Structure & Function

The body's structure and function are intimately related. When structure is compromised, function is affected throughout the entire system.

Self-Healing

The body has an inherent capacity for self-healing and self-regulation. Osteopathy supports and encourages these natural processes.

Whole Person Care

Treatment considers the whole person — body, mind, and spirit — not just the presenting symptoms or isolated body part.
